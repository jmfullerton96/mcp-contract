# Risk Assessment Framework

When evaluating risks for a target company, apply this structured framework. Each risk category should be assessed independently, then synthesized into an overall risk profile.

## Risk Categories

### Regulatory Risk
- Current regulatory environment and pending changes
- Compliance history (enforcement actions, consent decrees, fines)
- Exposure to regulatory regimes across operating jurisdictions

### Competitive Risk
- Market concentration and competitive positioning
- Barrier-to-entry dynamics and threat of new entrants
- Technology disruption potential within the sector

### Financial Risk
- Capital structure and leverage relative to peers
- Cash flow quality and sustainability
- Revenue concentration (customer, product, geography)
- Off-balance-sheet obligations and contingent liabilities

### Execution Risk
- Management track record on stated strategic initiatives
- Integration risk for recent or pending acquisitions
- Dependency on key personnel or critical suppliers

### Technological Risk
- Technical debt and infrastructure age
- Cybersecurity posture relative to industry standards
- Dependency on third-party platforms or services

## Severity Classification

Rate each identified risk on a three-tier scale:

| Level | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Material** | Could significantly impact valuation or deal viability. Requires mitigation plan or price adjustment. |
| **Notable** | Warrants monitoring and disclosure. Unlikely to block a transaction but affects risk premium. |
| **Background** | Industry-standard risk present across the peer set. No company-specific elevation. |

## Synthesis

After assessing individual categories, produce a risk summary that identifies:
1. The top 3 risks by severity
2. Any risk interactions (e.g., regulatory change that amplifies financial risk)
3. Recommended diligence follow-ups for material risks
